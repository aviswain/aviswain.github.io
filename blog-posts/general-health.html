Working Out

Currently I am optimizing for endurance, hypertrophy, and flexibility. I never got impressed by living heavy weights and honestly I'm quite scared of severely injuring myself. Despite that, I try to do compound movements at least once a week (Bench, Squat, Deadlift, and Bent-over Rows). Here is my current training split...

  • D1: Quad-Dominant Legs
  • D2: Chest
  • D3: Back and Biceps
  • D4: Abs and Lower Back
  • D5: Quads
  • D6: Hams and little bit of Back
  • D7: Chest
  • D8: Abs and Lower Back
  • repeat...

If I ever get a bad night of sleep, I do a 20-90 min Zone-2 cardio session instead of working out that day. I try to run outside because it's the easiest for me to get into 'Zone-2' quickly (I just measure Z2 as a pace where it's not enjoyable to keep a conversation at). But if I can't do that because of the weather, I use a stationary bike and do a longer session for around 60-90 mins since it takes me longer to get into Z2. My running sessions are normally around 20-45 mins.

Most times I prefer lifting weights over cardio, but I also hate going to the gym when I'm not in the mood. The gym is a nice break for me in the middle of the day (I normally workout 1-2 hours after breakfast), and I don't want to ruin it by dragging myself to do it on the days I get no sleep.

As per flexibility, I really should be working more on it. As of now, I just stretch and foam roll whenever I feel like it.

Eating

I have some general guidelines I follow when it comes to my nutrition. They have worked well for me and I don't see myself changing them drastically in the future...

  • I eat meals made at home as much as possible. I also avoid eating packaged foods and try to stick to whole foods. I love freshly made food!
  • I keep things balanced. Everyday I get some fruits, vegetables, carbs, fats, protein in. I don't count calories because that takes the fun out of it for me
  • I stay traditional. I mostly eat traditional dishes that have been a part of my family for many generations. If you think about it, your body is basically a product of natural selection across thousands of years. It just makes more sense to eat what your ancestors ate instead of anything else.
  • I don't eat immediately after waking up or immediately before going to sleep.

This is the overall mindset behind my nutritional choices. But here's some more details...

Pre-Breakfast

  • Foods: Water (18 ounces minimum)
  • Time: 7:00 AM

Breakfast:

  • Foods: Oatmeal, Banana, Berries, Nuts, Honey, Potatoes, Eggs, Kefir/Yogurt, Green Tea, Bread (rarely)
  • Time: Around 10:00 AM.
  • Usually somewhat heavy as its the meal before I workout. I focus on eating enough carbs.

Lunch:

  • Foods: Rice, Vegetables, Beans, Lentils, Chicken
  • Time: Around 1:00 - 2:00 PM
  • Most of the time this is my lightest meal. I eat it right after I workout. I eat enough to just get me by until dinner.

Dinner:

  • Foods: Rice, Vegetables, Beans, Lentils, Meat
  • Time: 7:00 - 8:00 PM. I aim for 2 hours before sleeping.
  • This is usually my heaviest meal. I like feeling full at the end of the day as it keeps me relaxed before sleep.

This is the typical routine for me. Every now and then I do get cravings for something savory or sugary. On those days I go out to eat with family and friends. I love the usual things - ice cream, burgers, pies, cake, pizza, cookies, etc. Unfortunately, when I choose eat desserts, I have the 'go big or go home' mindset. It's just how I'm wired. I do find that I get terrible sleep on those days and it messes up my next day, so I don't do it a lot (I end up relearning this lesson every 3 weeks or so).
